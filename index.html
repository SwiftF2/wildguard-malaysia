Why It Matters

Malaysia's Wildlife Is Under Threat

Rapid deforestation, poaching and the illegal wildlife trade are pushing Malaysia's most iconic species to the brink of extinction.

🌳

Deforestation Crisis

Malaysia has lost over 67% of its original forest cover since 1900, destroying the habitats of thousands of species that depend on these ecosystems.

🔫

Poaching & Wildlife Trade

Illegal poaching and the wildlife trade remain major threats, with species like the Malayan Tiger fetching high prices on black markets across Southeast Asia.

📢

Low Public Awareness

Despite the severity of the crisis, most Malaysians are unaware of how many species are endangered and what they can personally do to help.

Critically Endangered

Malayan Tiger

Panthera tigris jacksoni

Fewer than 150 individuals remain in the wild. The Malayan Tiger is a national symbol of Malaysia, yet faces extinction from habitat loss and poaching.

Endangered

Bornean Pygmy Elephant

Elephas maximus borneensis

The smallest elephant in Asia, found only in Sabah and Kalimantan. With only ~1,500 individuals left, their forest homes are rapidly disappearing.

Malayan Sun Bear
