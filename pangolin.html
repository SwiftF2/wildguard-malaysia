About Sunda Pangolin

The Sunda Pangolin (Manis javanica) is a remarkable and ancient mammal covered head to toe in overlapping scales made of keratin — the same material as human fingernails. It is the most widely distributed of the Asian pangolin species, found across Southeast Asia including Peninsular Malaysia and Borneo.

Pangolins are nocturnal, solitary, and highly secretive, making them difficult to study and monitor in the wild. When threatened, they curl into a tight ball, using their hard scales as armour — a defensive behaviour that, tragically, also makes them easy for poachers to pick up and bag.

The Sunda Pangolin holds the devastating distinction of being the most trafficked mammal in the world. Despite being legally protected in Malaysia and internationally under CITES Appendix I, tens of thousands of pangolins are poached annually to supply demand from illegal markets, primarily in China and Vietnam.

⚠️
Threats & Challenges
💊

Scales for Traditional Medicine

Pangolin scales are falsely believed to have medicinal properties and are highly sought after in illegal traditional medicine markets across Asia, despite having no proven medical benefit.

🍖

Bushmeat Demand

Pangolin meat is considered a luxury item in parts of China and Vietnam, driving extensive poaching and international trafficking of the species.

🌲

Habitat Loss

Deforestation and land conversion reduce the availability of the forest habitat that pangolins need for foraging and denning, making populations more vulnerable to hunting.

🪤

Snaring

Pangolins are frequently caught in wire snares set for other animals, contributing to population decline across their range in Southeast Asia.

🛡️
Conservation Efforts
🔍

Anti-Trafficking Enforcement

PERHILITAN and Malaysian customs work with international law enforcement to intercept pangolin shipments at ports and borders, seizing thousands of pangolins annually.

🏥

Rescue & Rehabilitation

Confiscated pangolins are rehabilitated and released back into protected forest areas. Several wildlife NGOs in Malaysia run specialised pangolin care programmes.

🧬

Population Research

Camera trap surveys and radio tracking studies are being conducted to understand pangolin population density, home range, and habitat use in Malaysian forests.

🌍

International Trade Bans

Malaysia actively supports CITES Appendix I listing for all pangolin species, banning all international commercial trade and pushing for stronger global enforcement.
