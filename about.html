Who We Are

Giving Malaysia's Wildlife a Digital Voice

WildGuard Malaysia was created as a Final Year Project for the Diploma in Multimedia programme at Management and Science University (MSU), aligned with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 15 — Life on Land.

The project was born from a simple observation: despite Malaysia being one of the world's most biodiverse nations, most Malaysians have very limited knowledge of how many of our wildlife species are endangered — or what they can personally do to help.

WildGuard Malaysia bridges this gap by creating a visually engaging, accessible, and community-driven digital platform that makes wildlife conservation feel personal, urgent, and achievable for every Malaysian.

SDG 15 — Life on Land

WildGuard Malaysia directly supports SDG 15, which calls on all nations to protect, restore and promote sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems, sustainably manage forests, combat desertification, halt and reverse land degradation and halt biodiversity loss.

How This Project Contributes

✓ Raises public awareness about endangered Malaysian wildlife species
✓ Connects users with established conservation organisations
✓ Encourages community pledges and direct conservation action
✓ Educates Malaysians on the impact of deforestation on biodiversity
Our Values

What Drives WildGuard Malaysia

🎯

Awareness First

We believe that knowledge is the foundation of action. WildGuard Malaysia makes complex conservation data accessible and understandable for everyone.

🤝

Community Action

Conservation is a collective effort. We connect individuals with organisations and empower every Malaysian to be part of the solution.

💡

Design for Impact

We use the power of design and multimedia to create emotional connections between people and wildlife — because caring leads to protecting.

🌿

Sustainability

Every design decision — from the content to the code — is made with long-term environmental awareness and responsible practices in mind.

🔬

Research-Based

All species profiles, conservation statistics, and news content are sourced from reputable organisations including IUCN, WWF-Malaysia, MYCAT, and PERHILITAN.

📱

Accessible to All

WildGuard Malaysia is designed to work beautifully on any device — desktop, tablet, or mobile — ensuring every Malaysian can access it.

Project Timeline

Week 2

Proposal Presentation

Two draft proposals presented to supervisor. WildGuard Malaysia selected as the FYP direction.

Weeks 3–6

Research & Rough Designing

Target audience research, competitor analysis, wireframe sketches, and site architecture planning.

Weeks 5–10

Design Process

Visual identity development, species card design, illustration creation, and UI component design in Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop.

Weeks 9–13

UI/UX Prototyping

High-fidelity interactive prototype in Adobe XD, responsive design for desktop and mobile, user testing and refinements.

Week 12

Final Exhibition

Final presentation and product exhibition to panel of evaluators and the public.

Week 14

Final Submission

Final draft report, complete report, and logbook submission to FYP coordinator.
